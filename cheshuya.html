Chapter 07:

Cheshuya

// The Scale

Someone slows.
“Finally,” someone says.
The surface breaks. A splash.
A brief, shiny flash of Cheshuya catches the light.
The fish turns and slips under.
Ripples spread in slow rings, thinning until the surface is still.

[ IDENTITY ]

ЧЕШУЯ • Cheshuya [che-shu-'ya]

[ CLASSIFICATION ]

Dermal plates / Biological armor.

[ TECHNICAL PROFILE ]

Small, rigid plates that grow out of the skin of a fish to provide protection and assist in locomotion.

[ PROPERTIES ]

Optical Reflection: Highly reflective surfaces create sudden, sharp flashes of light in otherwise turbid, light-absorbing water environments.

[ FIELD NOTES ]

Fish are an incredibly important part of Astrakhan. We would go on fishing trips for days, weeks at a time, in every season of the year. It feeds and binds people. It creates rituals and special knowledge, shaping people through their understanding of the rivers. There are a lot of fish, and also many swimming snakes. And there are myths about the water creatures in those muddy waters—like rough mermaids who don't even bother to seduce you like in other folklore; they just take you under. Or a fish so huge that if you sat on its head, your feet couldn't reach the floor.
