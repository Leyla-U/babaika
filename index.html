Тамариск (tamarisk)

halophytic woody shrub adapted to saline and alkaline soils. Uses salt-excreeting leaf glands to regulate ionic balance. Deep root systems allow survival in eroded ground. Phytosociologically dominant along the lower Volga banks.

The ground changes. Salt tightens the soil and limits what can live there. Most plants fall away.

Tamarisk remains.

Slender branches, light blooms, restrained color. It bends easily and does not retreat.

Туман (tuman)

advection fog formed when warm moist air moves over cooler water or land. Visibility reduces below 1 km. Temperature inversion traps moisture close to the surface. A seasonal phenomenon of the delta lowlands, most frequent in early morning.

Tuman spreads in the cold.

It covers land and water together - a soft, semi-transparent blanket - light, diffused, almost weightless. Colors mute and blur.

Полои (poloi)

seasonally inundated lowlands of the Volga-Akhtuba floodplain. Filled by spring floods, drained as waters recede. Support ephemeral wetland habitats. Characterised by silty soils and halophyte communities.

Beyond the road edge, water spreads outward into Poloi.

Shallow and still. The surface holds the sky evenly, smooth and reflective, blue resting undisturbed by current.

Камыш (kamysh)

Phragmites australis. Common reed. Rhizomatous perennial grass of wetland margins. Grows in dense monotypic stands 2–4m high. Stem structure creates vertical, rhythmic pattern. Seed heads persist through winter as dry feathery plumes.

The passage tightens. Kamish closes in.

Dense vertical stems rise in compressed layers, fibrous and dry, shifting in color from pale beige to muted green to deep shadow. The space becomes structured, enclosed, almost woven.

Ил (il)

fine-grained fluvial sediment deposited when current velocity drops below transport threshold. Particle size 2–62 μm. Accumulates in deltaic backwaters and oxbow lakes. High organic content. Fertile, stable, slow.

The soil changes to Il.

Underfoot, it yields, too soft to hold form, too soft to resist pressure. Warm, saturated, it extends into the water. Il settles where current slows, where lotus establishes and accumulates sediment.
