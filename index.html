ТУМАН • Tuman [tu-ˈman]

Meteorological phenomenon consisting of a dense suspension of microscopic water droplets in the atmospheric boundary layer. Functions as an optical attenuator, scattering visible light and reducing spatial depth.

01

ТАМАРИСК • Tamarisk

halophytic woody shrub adapted to saline and alkaline soils. Uses salt-excreeting leaf glands to regulate ionic balance. Deep root systems allow survival in eroded ground. Phytosociologically dominant along the lower Volga banks.

02

The ground changes. Salt tightens the soil and limits what can live there. Most plants fall away.

Tamarisk remains. Slender branches, light blooms, restrained color. It bends easily and does not retreat.

seasonally inundated lowlands of the Volga-Akhtuba floodplain. Filled by spring floods, drained as waters recede. Support ephemeral wetland habitats. Characterised by silty soils and halophyte communities.

ИЛ • Il

fine-grained fluvial sediment deposited when current velocity drops below transport threshold. Particle size 2–62 μm. Accumulates in deltaic backwaters and oxbow lakes. High organic content. Fertile, stable, slow.

04

The soil changes to Il. Underfoot, it yields, too soft to hold form, too soft to resist pressure. Warm, saturated, it extends into the water. Il settles where current slows, where lotus establishes and accumulates sediment.

РАСКАТЫ • Raskaty

Terminal shallow-water expanses of the Volga delta, formed where narrow, highly sinuous distributary channels (eriki) lose their structural banks. The eriki function as a dynamic hydrological labyrinth, transporting water and sediment before ultimately dissolving into the boundaryless aquatic gradient of the raskaty.

05

Someone sits into a boat. The river fractures toward the Raskaty, forming the narrow corridors of the eriki. The path slips sideways, then forward again. The turns resemble veins, branching and returning, readable only to those who have navigated them before.

КАМЫШ • Kamysh

Phragmites australis. Common reed. Rhizomatous perennial grass of wetland margins. Grows in dense monotypic stands 2–4m high. Stem structure creates vertical, rhythmic pattern. Seed heads persist through winter as dry feathery plumes.

06

The passage tightens. Kamish closes in. Dense vertical stems rise in compressed layers, fibrous and dry, shifting in color from pale beige to muted green to deep shadow. The space becomes structured, enclosed, almost woven.
